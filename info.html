How the TroLab Demo Works

The TroLab Demo is a simplified interface where users can simulate experiments by adjusting various natural forces and observing their combined outcomes:

  • Light Intensity: Represents the energy input and the informational signals carried by light.
  • Sound Frequency: Represents the structuring and organizing principles through vibration and resonance.
  • Water Flow: Represents the storage medium, acting as memory and processor for the system.
  • Mirror Angles (X, Y, Z): Control how the light is reflected, refracted, and redirected within the system, simulating the recursive feedback necessary for computation.

When you adjust these parameters and run the experiment, TroLab computes a result combining all these forces. It generates:

  • A unique RGB color based on the light, sound, and water values.
  • A mirror efficiency score based on the alignment of mirrors (simulating reflection quality and feedback strength).
  • A summary of experiment data, which you can log or download as a CSV file.

Results are displayed in graphs for Light, Sound, Water, and Mirror Efficiency, and color is shown in a visual box representing the final output of the experiment.
